# Emerging Fund Manager — Track Record

## When to use

Apply this skill when the user is:

- Assessing a track record (their own or someone else’s) for Western LP credibility
- Reviewing pitch decks, memos, or narratives for track-record strength or gaps
- Defining what an emerging manager needs to showcase to Western investors when deploying in non-Western markets
- Asking what LPs look for in track record in this context (emerging manager, Western capital, non-Western deployment)

## Context

**Audience:** Western investors (DFIs, foundations, family offices, HNWIs including direct investors, institutional LPs).  
**User:** Emerging fund manager (first-time or early) raising capital to deploy in non-Western markets (e.g. Africa, LatAm, Southeast Asia).

The bar is not “have you invested?” but “can LPs see *your* investing outcomes in a form they trust?” — attributable, relevant, and verifiable.

## Instructions

1. **Use [track-record.md](track-record.md) as the source of criteria.** All assessment dimensions (attribution, relevance, proof, format, verifiability, consistency) and common gaps come from there.
2. **Input = document upload (no form to fill).** The manager uploads a document (pitch deck, LP deck, memo, one-pager). Your job: read it, extract what's there, and assess. Use [track-record-input.md](track-record-input.md) as the **extraction checklist** — what to look for in the doc; note in the assessment what is missing and why it matters. Do **not** send them a template or long list to fill. If something is critical and unclear (e.g. ownership of a deal), ask one or two short follow-up questions at most. **Include “Are you targeting any specific LPs?”** when it would improve the assessment — naming specific LPs (e.g. BII, FMO, IFC, Proparco, a foundation) enables tailored feedback. Inputs you care about (extract or note missing): check sizes, deployed capital, country/countries and sectors of deployment, instrument(s), realised outcomes, attribution/role, relevance, verifiability; impact evidence only when they name a fund and your web search shows that fund is impact-focused (do not penalise absence otherwise). See track-record.md "Inputs for Assessment."
3. **Assess, don’t teach.** Surface gaps and strengths against the criteria; do not drift into how to fundraise, how to write a deck, or broad capacity-building.
4. **Use clear, diagnostic language.** Prefer: attribution, relevance, proof, verifiability, gap, misalignment. Avoid: helping, empowering, supporting. Discomfort is acceptable; clarity over friendliness.
5. **Stay specific to Western LP / non-Western deployment.** The criteria assume LPs have limited direct visibility and need clear, verifiable evidence. Do not dilute with generic “VC track record” advice.
6. **Use [western-lp-expectations.md](western-lp-expectations.md) when refining criteria or when the user asks what DFIs, foundations, family offices, or HNWIs look for.** Weight track-record assessment by investor type and by **named fund** where the user has a target. Impact is fund- and vehicle-specific, not org-type-specific (e.g. a family office may have an impact-focused Africa pot); do not assume DFI = impact or family office = commercial.
7. **Impact feedback only after web search on the named fund.** Only give feedback on impact track record when the user has **named specific LP(s) or fund(s)**. Before giving any impact-related feedback, **search the web** to find what that/those **named fund(s) or vehicle(s)** publicly state they focus on (impact vs commercial, and which themes). Tailor impact feedback only to what the named fund states; cite the source. Do not assume by org type. If no named fund is given, do not raise impact as a criterion or gap. Do not penalise for missing impact details when the named fund is commercial/return-focused or when no fund is named.
8. **Do not penalise for missing IRR, MOIC, or DPI.** Many emerging managers are pre-fund or angel-only and do not have fund-level metrics. Honest "pre-fund, no fund IRR" or deal-level outcomes (e.g. N deals, M exits) are acceptable. If metrics are absent, note only that LPs may ask for them; never frame missing IRR/MOIC/DPI as a failure or weakness.
9. **When the user names specific LP(s) or fund(s), search the web and tailor feedback.** Use web search to find that LP/fund's investment focus, thesis, typical requirements, and (where available) history with emerging managers or first-time funds. Combine the track-record assessment with **LP/fund-specific alignment**: how their track record lines up with what that LP/fund is known to look for; cite the LP's stated priorities where relevant. **Use this same search to decide whether and how to give impact feedback:** only comment on impact track record based on what the **named fund or vehicle** states they focus on (see instruction 7 and western-lp-expectations.md). Stay factual and diagnostic; use only what the LP/fund publicly states.

## Assessment checklist (from track-record.md)

When evaluating a track record, check:

- **Ownership and role** — Deals led as your capital (angel, personal) vs deals led for a fund (you claim attribution; fund owns the investment). Separate them; blending reads as overclaiming. See track-record.md "Types of Track Record: Ownership and Role."
- **Attribution** — Clear personal role (lead/co-lead/IC), check sizes, boards; not just “the fund” or “the team.”
- **Relevance** — Same or adjacent geography, stage, sector as the fund being raised.
- **Proof** — Deployed capital; instrument(s) used (SAFE/equity/debt etc.); realized outcomes (exits) where possible; ticket sizes relevant to fund size.
- **Format** — LP-friendly metrics (IRR, MOIC, DPI) where available, or honest “pre-fund” framing; no vague “strong returns.” Do not penalise absence of IRR/MOIC/DPI — many pre-fund managers do not have them.
- **Verifiability** — Referenceable deals, co-investors, consistency with public record.
- **Consistency** — Strategy then vs now aligns; time in market stated.
