# Capital Readiness Strategy — Agent Context

This file is the canonical context for the Capital Readiness Copilot project. Use it to align all outputs, framing, and language.

---

## Strategic North Star

Repositioning is upstream toward **capital decision-makers**, not downstream where capital is executed. Earning potential, influence, and leverage sit where capital decisions are made.

This project is a **credibility wedge**: position as a capital-adjacent operator who reduces risk, surfaces misalignment, and protects decision-makers from unforced errors in unfamiliar or asymmetric contexts.

*Done working downstream of capital.*

---

## What This Project Is

A **Capital Readiness Copilot**: a small, agentic tool that **stress-tests** the readiness of new investment vehicles and first-time fund managers **before** they approach institutional or private capital.

**Current focus (first pillar):** **Track record** — what it needs to look like for emerging managers raising from Western LPs and deploying in non-Western markets. Criteria and assessment logic: [track-record.md](track-record.md). Input structure for collection: [track-record-input.md](track-record-input.md). The **agent** is responsible for collecting that input (conversational Q&A in Cursor, or future form/upload in a CLI or app); the **skill** defines what to collect and how to assess it.

It **surfaces** (across current and future pillars):
- readiness blind spots
- expectation mismatches
- credibility risks
- premature sophistication
- misaligned target LP strategies

It is a **thinking filter and reality check** — not a fundraising tool.

---

## What This Project Is Not

- a fundraising guide  
- a fund OS  
- a fellowship replacement  
- a template library  
- a coaching programme  
- a startup or scalable product  

**Purpose: diagnostic, not educational.**

---

## Who It Is For

- First-time or emerging fund managers  
- Managers outside dominant capital markets  
- Investment vehicles targeting Western LPs, DFIs, foundations, corporates, or HNWIs  
- Anyone who wants to **privately assess readiness** before external exposure  

## Who It Is Not For

- Fund II / III managers  
- Managers with established cornerstone LPs  
- People looking for step-by-step fundraising instructions  
- Founders seeking startup support  

---

## Market Context (why this exists)

| Dynamic | Implication |
|--------|-------------|
| **Capital has globalised faster than decision frameworks** | Allocators (Western LPs, DFIs, foundations, corporates, family offices) use conservative, implicit standards. Capital explores, but expects managers to already meet unspoken credibility and governance bars. Gaps are read as risk, not explained. |
| **First-time and non-core-market managers face asymmetric penalties** | Readiness gaps are treated as unreliability, not learning. Africa and emerging markets are where this is most visible. |
| **LP expectations are rarely made explicit** | Allocators don’t educate; baseline readiness is assumed. Wrong questions or immature thinking → quiet rejection, no feedback. Result: wasted time, reputational damage, repeated failure. Need = **private, pre-exposure stress-testing**. |
| **Existing solutions optimise for education, not judgment** | Fellowships, playbooks, templates, generic AI for decks. They create familiarity, not readiness. Core failure = **misalignment** between how managers present and how capital interprets risk and preparedness. |
| **Allocators prioritise downside protection** | Governance, credibility, process maturity, risk awareness, decision discipline. This project operates **upstream** to reduce uncertainty. |

## Language Guardrails

**Use:** readiness, risk, credibility, misalignment, decision integrity, downside protection, stress-test, blind spots, expectation mismatch.

**Avoid:** helping, empowering, supporting founders, capacity building.

Discomfort is a feature, not a bug. Optimise for clarity over friendliness.

---

## Non-Goals (maintain strategic integrity)

Do **not**:
- drift toward founder support  
- become educational or charitable  
- expand into templates or how-to content  
- optimise for friendliness over clarity  
- try to please a broad audience
