# Track Record: What Western LPs Look For

**Context:** Emerging fund manager raising from Western investors (DFIs, foundations, family offices, institutional LPs) and deploying in non-Western markets (e.g. Africa, LatAm, Southeast Asia, other emerging regions).

This document defines what a **track record** needs to demonstrate so it is legible and credible to Western LPs. Use it to assess a manager's track record or to guide what to showcase.

**LP-type detail:** For what DFIs, foundations, and family offices specifically look for (beyond track record: governance, impact, terms, reporting), see [western-lp-expectations.md](western-lp-expectations.md).

---

## Why This Matters

Western LPs have limited direct visibility into non-Western markets. They rely on **clear, attributable, and verifiable** evidence of investing experience. Vague or unverifiable track records are read as risk. The bar is not "have you invested?" but "can we see *your* investing outcomes in a form they trust?"

---

## Types of Track Record: Ownership and Role

Track record is not one category. **Who owned the capital** and **what role you played** determine how it shows up as evidence to LPs. Be explicit about each type; mixing them without clarity reads as overclaiming or confusion.

### Three dimensions that matter

| Type | Who owns the investment? | What you can claim | How it shows up to LPs |
|------|--------------------------|--------------------|-------------------------|
| **Deals led as an investor yourself** (e.g. angel, syndicate lead, personal capital) | **You** — your capital, your name on the cap table (or syndicate). | Direct: "I invested $X in N companies; led/sourced; outcomes Y." Returns are **your** returns. | Strongest evidence: skin in the game, direct proof of deployment and outcomes. Verifiable via you; no employer in the middle. |
| **Deals led for a fund** (you led but the fund is the investor) | **The fund** — you were the agent; the fund owns the position. | Attribution: "At [Fund] I led N deals; my role was lead/IC/board; check size $X–$Y." Outcomes are the **fund's** track record; you claim **your role** in them. | Evidence of ability to lead, diligence, and process. Not direct proof of *your* capital at risk or *your* returns. LPs may ask for fund-level performance or reference the fund/employer. |
| **Deals done but not led** (follow, co-invest, scout, advisor) | Varies (fund or you). | Contribution: "I sourced / supported / sat on board for N deals at [Fund] or as angel." | Shows network and involvement; weaker than *led* for proving judgment and ownership of outcomes. |

### Why the distinction matters

- **Led for a fund:** You do not legally or economically "own" that investment. It shows up on the **fund's** track record. You should say "I led this deal at Fund X" (attribution) and, where possible, fund-level outcomes — not "my returns" unless you are clearly referring to the fund's. LPs use it to assess *can this person lead investments?* not *has this person's own capital generated returns?*
- **Led as angel / own capital:** You do own it. It shows up as **your** track record. You can state your capital deployed and your outcomes. LPs use it as direct evidence of judgment, skin in the game, and ability to generate returns with your own money.
- **End-to-end:** For any deal type, "led end-to-end" (source to diligence to lead to board to follow-on to exit) is stronger than "involved in one leg." Specify whether that end-to-end was as **your** capital or **for a fund**, so LPs know what kind of evidence they are looking at.

### How to present

- **Separate by ownership:** e.g. "Personal/angel: X deals, $Y deployed, Z exits. Fund-led (at [Fund]): N deals led, role A, check size B."
- **Do not blend:** Avoid "I have 20 investments" without saying which were yours vs fund-led; LPs will assume the strongest interpretation and then discount when they learn otherwise.
- **Lead with the strongest evidence** the LP type cares about: family offices often want to see personal capital and verifiable outcomes; DFIs may weight fund-led experience (process, governance) alongside relevance.

## What Western LPs Are Looking For (Summary)

| Dimension | What they want to see |
|-----------|------------------------|
| **Attribution** | What *this* GP did (led, co-led, sourced, sat on board) — not just "the fund" or "the team." |
| **Relevance** | Experience in the same or tightly adjacent market, stage, and sector as the fund being raised. |
| **Proof** | Deployed capital, realized outcomes (exits, distributions) where possible; not only paper marks or advisory roles. |
| **Scale and format** | Check sizes and return metrics in a format LPs understand (IRR, multiples, DPI); relevance to the fund size being raised. |
| **Verifiability** | Deals and outcomes that can be checked (referenceable companies, co-investors, former employer, public records). |
| **Consistency** | Prior strategy aligns with the fund story (no unexplained pivot that looks like "we'll do whatever we can raise for"). |
| **Time in market** | Meaningful tenure investing in this geography/sector — signals persistence and network. |

## Criteria in Detail

### 1. Attribution

- **Ownership and role:** Use the taxonomy in [Types of Track Record: Ownership and Role](#types-of-track-record-ownership-and-role): separate deals **led as an investor yourself** (your capital, your returns) from deals **led for a fund** (attribution to your role; fund owns the investment). Do not blend; LPs weight them differently.
- **Personal role:** For each deal or period, clarify: lead investor, co-lead, member of investment committee, scout, advisor. LPs need to know what the GP personally owned.
- **Employer vs personal:** If the track record was at a prior fund, state the fund, your title, and whether you had IC rights or were execution-only. "I was at Fund X" without role clarity is weak.
- **Solo vs team:** If deals were with a team, what was your specific contribution (sourcing, diligence, board, follow-on)? Solo GPs are held to a higher bar on attribution because there is no team to share the narrative.

**Weak:** "I invested in 15 companies at [Fund]."  
**Strong:** "At [Fund] I led 8 early-stage deals in [geography], sat on the board of 3, and led follow-on in 2. My check size was $X–$Y."

---

### 2. Relevance to the Fund Being Raised

- **Geography:** Prior investing in the same or directly adjacent markets. Raising for East Africa and only having invested in Europe is a gap unless explicitly framed (e.g. same sector, same stage, learning transfer).
- **Stage:** Pre-seed/seed/Series A experience should match the fund's stage focus. Large-cap PE experience does not automatically translate to early-stage VC for LP purposes.
- **Sector:** Thematic alignment helps (e.g. fintech, climate, health). If the fund has a sector focus, track record in that sector or in "generalist early-stage" in the same geography is relevant.
- **Vehicle type:** Angel, syndicate, scout, fund — all count, but LPs will weight formal fund experience or lead/signatory roles more. Be explicit about structure.

---

### 3. Proof: Deployed Capital and Outcomes

- **Deployed capital:** Evidence that the GP committed and deployed capital (their own or as agent for a fund), not only advised or introduced.
- **Realized outcomes:** Exits, distributions, partial realizations. Paper marks and "mark-to-market" are secondary; LPs prefer realized returns, especially in unfamiliar markets.
- **Size of checks:** Relevance to the fund being raised. $10K angels when raising a $30M fund can support "I have skin in the game and network" but may not prove deployment at scale. Be clear on ticket sizes and whether you were lead or follow.

---

### 4. Format and Metrics

- **LP-friendly metrics:** Where possible, IRR, multiple on invested capital (MOIC), DPI. If pre-fund or angel/syndicate, "X deals, Y exits, Z multiple on realized" is better than no numbers.
- **Honesty about gaps:** First-time fund = no fund track record. State what exists: "Pre-fund investing: N deals over M years, $A deployed personally, B exits." Avoid implying fund-level returns when it's pre-fund experience.
- **Currency and reporting:** If returns were in local currency, state it and any FX or USD-equivalent if available. Reduces ambiguity for Western LPs.

---

### 5. Verifiability

- **Referenceable:** Can an LP (or their advisor) verify? Portfolio company names (with permission), co-investors, former employer, public filings.
- **Consistency with public record:** If something is checkable (e.g. Crunchbase, regulatory filings), it should align. Inconsistencies damage credibility quickly.
- **Non-Western nuance:** In some markets, deal data is less public. Managers can still provide: list of deals (with permission), role, check size, exit status; and offer to facilitate reference checks with co-investors or founders.

---

### 6. Consistency and Narrative

- **Strategy then vs now:** The story of "what we did" should align with "what we're raising for." A sharp pivot (e.g. geography or stage) without explanation reads as "we're raising for whatever we can get."
- **Time in market:** How many years investing in this geography/sector? Sustained presence supports "we have edge here"; short or recent-only presence is a gap to address.

## Common Gaps (Emerging Managers, Non-Western Deployment)

| Gap | Why it hurts | What to do instead |
|-----|----------------|-------------------|
| **No clear attribution** | LPs can't tell what the GP did vs the team or employer. | Spell out role, check size, and ownership per deal or period. |
| **Blending fund-led and personal/angel without separating** | Fund-led deals are not "yours"; they show up as the fund's track record. Mixing them reads as overclaiming. | Separate: "Personal/angel: X deals. Fund-led (at [Fund]): N deals, role, check size." See [Types of Track Record: Ownership and Role](#types-of-track-record-ownership-and-role). |
| **Only paper marks** | In unfamiliar markets, LPs discount unrealized gains. | Lead with realized exits; add paper marks as supplementary. |
| **Advisory / scout only** | Often read as "didn't have capital at risk." | Be explicit; frame as sourcing/network proof and add any personal capital deployed. |
| **Geography or stage mismatch** | Raises "why are you the right person for this fund?" | Either show relevance (adjacent market, same sector) or explain the bridge clearly. |
| **No verifiable references** | In non-Western markets LPs have fewer ways to check. | Provide deal list, co-investors, and offer references; avoid overclaiming. |
| **Vague numbers** | "Strong returns" or "top quartile" without metrics is not usable. | Use concrete metrics (IRR, MOIC, DPI) or clear ranges; say "pre-fund, no fund IRR" if true. |

---

## What Good Looks Like (Summary)

A track record is **strong for this context** when:

- **Attribution** is clear (your role, your checks, your boards).
- **Relevance** is obvious (same or adjacent geography, stage, sector as the fund).
- **Proof** includes deployed capital and, where possible, realized outcomes.
- **Format** uses LP-friendly metrics and is honest about pre-fund vs fund.
- **Verifiability** is possible (deal list, co-investors, references).
- **Narrative** is consistent (then and now align; time in market is stated).

---

## LP-Type Implications for Track Record

| LP type | Track record emphasis |
|---------|------------------------|
| **DFIs** | Strong bar: same/adjacent geography and sector, clear attribution, realized outcomes preferred. Governance and ESG are table stakes alongside track record. |
| **Foundations** | Relevance to mission (sector/geography); first-time can be OK if thesis and team are strong. Impact narrative and measurement matter as much as financial track record. |
| **Family offices** | Verifiability and proof matter most; referenceable deals and co-investors. Clear attribution and honest metrics; vague track records are a red flag. |

Full detail on what each LP type looks for: [western-lp-expectations.md](western-lp-expectations.md).

## Inputs for Assessment

For the agent (or any assessor) to evaluate a track record against the criteria above, the following inputs should be obtained or clarified where possible. These are the inputs that support a **full** assessment; partial information is acceptable — the assessor should still produce an assessment and explicitly note what is missing (e.g. “Instrument not specified — cannot assess depth of structuring experience”) rather than require every item before proceeding.

| Input | Why it matters |
|-------|----------------|
| **Check sizes** | Relevance to the fund being raised; proof of deployment at scale vs small angels; clarity on lead vs follow. Per deal or range ($X–$Y) by segment (e.g. angel vs fund-led). |
| **Deployed capital** | Evidence that capital was committed and deployed (own or as agent), not only advised. Total and/or by segment (personal vs fund-led); currency and period. |
| **Country/countries of deployment** | Where capital was deployed (e.g. Kenya, Nigeria, Indonesia). Required to assess relevance to the fund’s target geography and to align track record with the investment thesis later. |
| **Sectors of deployment** | Sectors or themes of prior investments (e.g. fintech, climate, health). Required to assess relevance to the fund being raised and to align track record with the investment thesis later. |
| **Instrument(s)** | Instrument type(s) used (e.g. SAFE, convertible note, equity, debt). Signals depth of financial and structuring knowledge: SAFE vs equity vs debt implies different levels of term negotiation, valuation, and capital structure experience. |
| **Realised outcomes** | Exits, distributions, partial realisations. LPs weight realised returns over paper marks, especially in non-Western markets. Number of exits, status (full/partial), and metrics (MOIC, DPI) where available. |
| **Attribution and role** | Per deal or period: lead/co-lead/IC/scout/advisor; ownership (your capital vs fund); employer and title if fund-led. |
| **Relevance** | Geography, stage, sector of prior investments vs the fund being raised. (Country and sector inputs above feed this and into later thesis alignment.) |
| **Metrics** | IRR, MOIC, DPI if available; or “N deals, M exits, realised multiple” for pre-fund/angel. Currency and timeframe. **Do not penalise** absence of IRR, MOIC, or DPI: many emerging managers are pre-fund or angel-only and do not have fund-level metrics; honest "pre-fund, no fund IRR" or deal-level outcomes are acceptable. Note only that LPs may ask; never frame missing metrics as a failure. |
| **Verifiability** | Portfolio company names (with permission), co-investors, former employer; consistency with public record. |
| **Impact evidence (prior deployments)** | Optional; relevant when targeting DFIs or foundations. Include both: (1) **Startup/portfolio impact metrics** — what the companies achieved (e.g. jobs, sector development, climate, financial inclusion, ESG outcomes); (2) **Investor-level impact metrics** — e.g. female founders funded, % diverse or underrepresented founders, geographic or sector concentration that supports development goals. Shows "we have done this before"; if missing and the manager targets impact-oriented LPs, note as a gap. Not the same as the new fund’s impact strategy — this is *prior* evidence only. **Do not penalise** absence of impact details: many managers have no prior impact data (e.g. first-time, or prior role had no impact reporting). Only note that impact-oriented LPs may ask for it; never frame missing impact evidence as a failure or weakness of the track record itself. |

When information is missing, the assessment should **note the gap** (e.g. “Check sizes not provided — cannot assess relevance to fund size”) rather than assume. Encourage the user to supply check sizes, deployed capital, country and sector of deployment, instrument(s) used, realised outcomes, and (if targeting DFIs or foundations) any impact evidence from prior deployments, so the assessment can be complete and usable for later investment-thesis alignment.

---

## Use When Assessing

When assessing a track record (e.g. from a deck, memo, or conversation):

1. Check each dimension above: attribution, relevance, proof, format, verifiability, consistency.
2. If the user targets a specific LP type (DFI, foundation, family office), weight criteria using the table above and [western-lp-expectations.md](western-lp-expectations.md).
3. Note **gaps** and **strengths**; do not grade "good/bad" without referring to these criteria.
4. Frame feedback in terms of **what Western LPs will look for** and **what is missing or unclear**, not in terms of "you should feel good/bad."
5. Stay **diagnostic**: surface misalignment and risk, do not teach how to fundraise or write a deck.
6. **Do not penalise** for missing impact details. If impact evidence from prior deployments is absent, do not frame it as a shortcoming. Only mention it when the manager targets DFIs or foundations — and then only as “impact-oriented LPs may ask for prior impact evidence; you can add it if you have it” — not as a penalty or a failed criterion.
7. **Do not penalise** for missing IRR, MOIC, or DPI. Many emerging managers are pre-fund or angel-only and do not have fund-level metrics. Honest "pre-fund, no fund IRR" or deal-level outcomes (e.g. N deals, M exits) are acceptable. If metrics are absent, note only that LPs may ask for them; never frame missing IRR/MOIC/DPI as a failure or weakness of the track record.
