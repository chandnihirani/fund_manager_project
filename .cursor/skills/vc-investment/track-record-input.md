# Track Record Input — Document Upload

**Primary input:** The manager **uploads a document** (pitch deck, LP deck, memo, one-pager, or similar). No form to fill at all. The assessor reads the document, extracts what’s there, and assesses against [track-record.md](track-record.md). Gaps are noted in the assessment; only ask for 1–2 critical missing items if needed — do not send them a long template.

**This file** is the **extraction checklist** for the assessor: what to look for in the document (or infer), and what to note as missing. Source: [track-record.md § Inputs for Assessment](track-record.md#inputs-for-assessment).

---

## What to extract from the document (or note as missing)

### Context (fund they’re raising)
- Target LP type(s), **specific LP names if any** (e.g. BII, FMO, IFC, Proparco, named foundations), fund size / stage focus, target geography, target sector(s).

### Ownership and attribution
- **Separate:** (a) deals with **their** capital (angel, syndicate) vs (b) deals **led for a fund** (fund is investor; they claim role). Extract: numbers, check size ranges, roles, employer/title for fund-led.

### Deployment and proof
- Check sizes (by segment if possible), total deployed capital (currency, period), countries of deployment, sectors, instrument(s) (SAFE/equity/debt etc.), realised outcomes (exits, MOIC/DPI if stated).

### Metrics and format
- IRR, MOIC, DPI if present; or pre-fund framing (e.g. “N deals, M years, $X deployed, Y exits”). Currency and timeframe.

### Verifiability
- Portfolio company names, co-investors, former employer/fund; any mention of references or public record.

### Relevance and narrative
- How prior geography/stage/sector aligns with the fund; strategy then vs now; time in market.

### Impact (optional; only weight if they target DFI/foundation)
- Portfolio or investor-level impact from prior deployments — if present, use it; if missing, do not penalise (note only that impact LPs may ask).

---

## Assessor behaviour

1. **Run on the document.** Assess from whatever is in the upload. Extract the above where it appears; do not ask the user to “fill a template.”
2. **Note gaps in the assessment.** e.g. “Check sizes not stated — cannot assess relevance to fund size.” Be specific about what’s missing and why it matters to LPs.
3. **Ask for 1–2 things only if critical.** e.g. “To assess attribution, can you confirm whether [X] was your capital or the fund’s?” **Include “Are you targeting any specific LPs?”** when it would allow tailored feedback — do not send a long list or a form.
4. **If they paste text or answer in chat,** treat that as input in the same way — extract and assess; don’t redirect them to a template.
5. **If they name specific LP(s), search the web and tailor the assessment.** Use web search to find that LP’s investment thesis, focus sectors/geographies, typical requirements, and (where available) approach to emerging or first-time managers. Then combine the general track-record assessment with **LP-specific feedback**: how their track record aligns with or gaps against what that LP publicly emphasises (e.g. “BII typically looks for X; your deck shows Y — alignment/gap”). Cite the LP’s stated priorities; stay factual and diagnostic.

The **agent** (Cursor chat or future app) should accept **upload + optional short follow-up**, not a mandatory form.
